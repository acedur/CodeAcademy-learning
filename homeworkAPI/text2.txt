<p>On Tuesday, September 6, <span class="interwiki-link-foreign"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cedar_Point" class="extiw" title="w:Cedar Point">Cedar Point</a></span> amusement park in <a href="/wiki/Ohio" class="mw-redirect" title="Ohio">Ohio</a>, <a href="/wiki/United_States" class="mw-redirect" title="United States">United States</a> announced that <span class="interwiki-link-foreign"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Top_Thrill_Dragster" class="extiw" title="w:Top Thrill Dragster">Top Thrill Dragster</a></span>, the second tallest-roller coaster in the world, has permanently closed. The ride was the first full-circut <span class="interwiki-link-forced-foreign"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/rollercoaster#Strata_coaster" class="extiw" title="w:rollercoaster">strata coaster</a></span>, a roller coaster exceeding a 400&#160;feet (122&#160;m) height limit, in the world.
</p><p>Director of communications at Cedar Point, Tony Clark, released a statement that "after 19 seasons in operation ... the world's first strata coaster, Top Thrill Dragster ... is being retired."
</p><p>The news comes after an accident on August 15, 2021, when an L-shaped bracket fell on the head of a guest in the queue line, causing a serious brain injury and leading to the closure of the attraction for the rest of the 2021 and 2022 seasons. The bracket was later identified as a sensor used to detect where the cars were on the track.
</p><p>The park was cleared by Ohio officials in February 2022 of any wrong-doing or of having reason to believe that the ride was unsafe before operation. Prior to the August 2021 accident, two guest-related accidents occurred on the attraction.
</p><p>Although the ride has been retired in its current form, park officials have stated that they are working on "creating a new and reimagined ride experience." It was not confirmed if it was the accident that led to the permanent closure of the attraction.
